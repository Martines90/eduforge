### PRIMARY SOURCES
**Definition**: Created at the time by people who experienced the events

**Types**:
- Letters, diaries, memoirs
- Government documents, treaties, laws
- Newspapers from the period
- Speeches, declarations
- Photographs, artifacts
- Statistical records

**Analysis Questions**:
- Who created this? Why?
- What bias might they have?
- What does this reveal about the time?
- How reliable is this source?

---

### CAUSATION ANALYSIS
Understanding WHY events happened

**Elements**:
- Immediate causes (triggers)
- Long-term causes (underlying conditions)
- Multiple factors interacting
- Unintended consequences

**Real Applications**: Policy decisions, conflict resolution, predicting outcomes

---

### CHANGE & CONTINUITY
What changed vs. what stayed the same

**Focus**:
- Political systems
- Economic structures
- Social norms
- Technological advancement
- Cultural values

---

### COMPARISON
Analyzing similarities and differences

**Types**:
- Different time periods (same place)
- Different places (same time)
- Different perspectives on same event
- Different responses to similar challenges

---

### HISTORICAL INTERPRETATION
Understanding different viewpoints

**Elements**:
- Contemporary perspectives (people at the time)
- Modern interpretations (historians later)
- Bias and propaganda
- Changing narratives over time

---

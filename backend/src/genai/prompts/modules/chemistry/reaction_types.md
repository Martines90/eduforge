## 4. ‚öóÔ∏è CHEMICAL COMPOSITION, INTERACTIONS & TRANSFORMATIONS

### How Chemical Systems Behave and Change:

#### **A. CHEMICAL REACTIONS & TRANSFORMATIONS**
- **Synthesis Reactions**: Building complex molecules from simpler ones
- **Decomposition**: Breaking down compounds (thermal, electrolytic, catalytic)
- **Single/Double Displacement**: Ion exchange, precipitation reactions
- **Combustion**: Oxidation releasing energy (fires, explosions, metabolism)
- **Redox**: Electron transfer (batteries, corrosion, metallurgy)

#### **B. REACTION KINETICS & RATES**
- **Factors Affecting Rate**: Temperature, concentration, catalysts, surface area
- **Half-Life**: Radioactive decay (Polonium-210: 138 days), drug metabolism
- **Reaction Mechanisms**: Step-by-step pathways, rate-determining steps
- **Catalysis**: Lowering activation energy (enzyme catalysis, industrial catalysts)

#### **C. CHEMICAL EQUILIBRIUM & SHIFTS**
- **Le Chatelier's Principle**: System response to stress (pressure, temperature, concentration changes)
- **Solubility**: Precipitation, dissolution, common ion effect
- **Acid-Base**: pH changes, buffer action, titration endpoints
- **Industrial Optimization**: Maximizing product yield (Haber process: high pressure favors NH‚ÇÉ)

#### **D. ENERGY CHANGES IN REACTIONS**
- **Exothermic**: Release energy (combustion, neutralization, crystallization)
- **Endothermic**: Absorb energy (photosynthesis, thermal decomposition, dissolving NH‚ÇÑNO‚ÇÉ)
- **Bond Energy**: Breaking bonds (requires energy), forming bonds (releases energy)
- **Thermochemical Calculations**: q = mcŒîT, Hess's Law, ŒîH calculations

   - YES: NASA engineer, toxicologist, forensic chemist, farmer, physician, alchemist, artisan

2. **Create GENUINE NEED** for chemical calculations
   - Critical decision with consequences (lives, safety, success/failure)
   - Problem that ONLY chemistry can solve
   - Real-world or historical application

3. **Provide SPECIFIC CHEMICAL DATA**
   - Real formulas (not "Compound A")
   - Concrete values (masses, volumes, concentrations, temperatures)
   - Relevant constants (molar masses, R, F, half-lives)
   - Balanced equations or reactants/products to balance

4. **Demand CALCULATION & ANALYSIS**, not just recall
   - Apply stoichiometry, gas laws, pH, thermochemistry, etc.
   - Show work with units
   - Interpret results in context
   - Make decisions from calculations

5. **Match CHEMISTRY TYPE to CURRICULUM TOPIC**
   - Stoichiometry topic ‚Üí Mass/mole/volume calculations
   - Acid-base topic ‚Üí pH, neutralization, titration
   - Gas laws topic ‚Üí PV=nRT applications
   - Nuclear topic ‚Üí Half-life, decay calculations

6. **Show CONSEQUENCES** of chemical knowledge
   - What happens if Apollo 13 calculation wrong? (Astronauts die from CO‚ÇÇ)
   - What if Chernobyl engineer adds water to graphite? (Explosion)
   - What if gold miner miscalculates cyanide dose? (Lethal poisoning)
